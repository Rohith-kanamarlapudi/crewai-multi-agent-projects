- **Subscription transition** driving margin expansion

This diversification means Broadcom can continue generating predictable revenue and cash flow **even if the semiconductor cycle turns** — a critical advantage that neither Snowflake (pure-play SaaS, vulnerable to consumption volatility) nor Palantir (momentum-driven multiple) can claim.

- **Revenue uncorrelated to individual chip customer success**
- **Incremental growth** with every new data center buildout

This is classic "picks and shovels" positioning in a gold rush — and Broadcom holds the monopoly on shovels.

- **Decades of successful M&A integration** (Avago/Broadcom, VMware, CA Technologies, Symantec)

The market can trust that Broadcom will continue to execute and create shareholder value — a governance advantage that cannot be overstated.

### 6. Reasonable Valuation Relative to Growth

While no large-cap AI stock is "cheap," Broadcom's valuation is the most justifiable:

- **Forward P/E** in the mid-to-high 20s/low 30s

## PART II: WHY SNOWFLAKE WAS NOT SELECTED

### 1. Growth is Good, But Not Best-in-Class

Snowflake's **34% product revenue growth** is impressive for a $5.5B+ business, but it lags behind:

- Broadcom's 106% AI chip growth
- Palantir's 48-53% overall growth (93% U.S. commercial)

In an environment where AI tailwinds are creating once-in-a-decade growth opportunities, 34% is **respectable but not exceptional**.

### 2. Competitive Pressure from Databricks is Real

Databricks is growing at **~55% YoY** — significantly faster than Snowflake — and is increasingly encroaching on Snowflake's core data warehousing market with its unified analytics and AI platform. Key concerns:

- Databricks has **stronger AI/ML native capabilities** (built on Apache Spark)
- **Open-source ecosystem** (Delta Lake, MLflow) creates lower switching costs
- **Larger AI-data-native mindshare** in the developer community
- Both companies are targeting the same enterprise customers

This competitive dynamic means Snowflake's moat is **narrower than it appears**.

### 3. Consumption Model Volatility

Snowflake's usage-based pricing model, while powerful for expansion (126% NRR), also creates:

- **Revenue unpredictability** — customer usage can fluctuate quarter-to-quarter
- **Sensitivity to macro conditions** — enterprises can cut usage more easily than fixed contracts
- **Guidance misses** can trigger severe multiple compression (as seen in early 2024)

### 4. Lower Margin Profile

Snowflake's operating margins, while improving, remain below Broadcom's:

- Snowflake: ~25-30% operating margins (improving)
- Broadcom: ~41% operating margins (best-in-class for diversified tech)

At Snowflake's scale (~$5.5B revenue), investors expect clearer paths to Rule of 40 excellence relative to growth.

### 5. Valuation is Elevated for the Growth Rate

Snowflake trades at a premium multiple relative to its ~34% growth rate. While the AWS partnership and Cortex AI provide upside optionality, the risk/reward is **less favorable than Broadcom's**, where 106% growth supports the multiple more directly.

### 2. Momentum-Driven Stock Vulnerability

Palantir has been the **best-performing S&P 500 stock** (+110% YTD), which often signals:

- **Crowded positioning** — many holders with no fundamental anchoring
- **Mean reversion risk** — the best-performing stocks frequently underperform in subsequent periods
- **Extreme expectations** — any slight miss could trigger a 30-50% drawdown

### 3. Insider Selling Signals

**Peter Thiel and other insiders have been net sellers**, with 95+ sells vs. limited buys in recent months. While insider selling is not always a negative signal, the **magnitude and consistency** raise questions about whether those closest to the business see the current valuation as sustainable.

### 4. Competition is Intensifying

Palantir's success is attracting powerful competitors:

- **Microsoft** — investing heavily in Azure AI and Copilot for defense/government
- **Databricks** — expanding into operational AI applications
- **Defense primes** (Lockheed Martin, Raytheon, Northrop Grumman) — building AI capabilities in-house
- **Amazon** — AWS is increasingly offering competing services

While Palantir's moat in government is real, it is **not impenetrable**, and competition is growing.

### 5. The Commercial "Proof" is Early

Yes, U.S. commercial revenue grew 93% YoY — but from a **small base** ($306M in Q2 2025). The question is whether this growth rate is sustainable as the base grows and early adopters are exhausted. Palantir has not yet proven it can sustain these growth rates at scale.

### 6. The Business is Real, But the Price is the Problem

I want to be clear: **Palantir may be an excellent business with a bright future.** The $10B Army contract, AIP adoption, and 93% commercial growth are genuine achievements. However, **even great businesses can be terrible investments at the wrong price.**

At its current valuation, Palantir needs to grow into its multiple for years without any mistakes, competitive setbacks, or macro headwinds. That is an exceptionally high-risk bet.
